About The School

The Romero brothers have been involved in the sport of boxing for more than thirty years. They are five brothers, Jeik, Eric, Raul, Ricardo and Anthony. The Romero’s were born in Puerto Rico, in a town named Carolina, known as La Tierra de Gigantes, which means the land of giants. Boxing is very popular in Puerto Rico. Many people do not know that Puerto Rico has more world champions per capita than anywhere else on the planet. The Romero brothers grew up in a boxing environment. Jeik, the oldest brother, started boxing at the age of 14. They all remembered when Jeik used to train and work out, because he always makes sure his brothers were around so they can join him too. Even as little kids, they enjoyed doing so, and this is how the Romero’s fall in love with the boxing sport. In 1998, Jeik, Raul Ricardo and Anthony came to live to Madison Wisconsin. By then, Jeik the oldest brother, was not boxing, but he was very busy training his brothers. Jeik was in their corners coaching them, and out of the ring always making sure they were focus training and going to the gym every day. Few years later, in 2001, Anthony, won the championship boxing state tournament, and Ricardo won the golden glove tournament. In 2001 and 2002, Raul won back-to-back the Golden Glove tournament, and also went to represent the state of Wisconsin in the National boxing tournament. Today, the Romero’s are actually training their sons and nephews, and this is one of the main reasons why they decided to open the Romero’s Boxing School. It has made them realized that training boxing is what they want to do for the rest of their lives. Due to the knowledge and vast experience the Romero brothers have in the sport of boxing, they are very confident they will be successful in teaching anyone who wants to learn about the art and science of boxing.
